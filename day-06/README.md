## Context: My Detailed Reflection

### 📖 What I Learned

- **On-chain identity starts with a keypair.** A Solana keypair gives you a Public Key (your address, visible to everyone) and a Private Key (your proof of ownership, never to be shared).
- **Public Key = your social media handle.** It acts as your identity across the entire Solana ecosystem — not just one app.
- **Private Key = your password, but stronger.** Unlike a Web2 password, no one can reset it for you. There is no "Forgot Password" on Solana.
- **You truly own your identity.** In Web2, your account exists because a company stores your credentials — they can lock you out. On Solana, only the holder of the private key can sign transactions. No company, no admin panel, no override.
- **One identity, every app.** Your keypair works across every application on the Solana network without asking anyone's permission. Tokens, votes, reputation — all tied to the same keypair.

---

### 🧱 Challenges Faced

- **Writing fear.** I have had a fear of writing since school. This was the first time I approached it as a process rather than a performance — breaking it into hook, bridge, reveal, and close.
- **Finding the right analogy.** Several ideas came to mind — government identity systems, SSH keys, social media accounts. Choosing the right one for a Web2 developer audience took deliberate thought.
- **The "so what" problem.** It was easy to explain _what_ a keypair is. It was harder to explain _why it matters_. The answer: one identity, every app, no permissions needed.
- **Ending the post.** Closing lines are deceptively hard. The trick was to callback the opening — _Meri chabi, meri pehchan_ — so the reader feels the loop close with new understanding.
