I'm Denil Bhatt — a developer who's previously shipped on **Ethereum** and **Polygon**, and picked up this challenge out of pure curiosity to explore Solana after about a year away from blockchain development.

This repo is my public learning log for the **[MLH 100 Days of Solana](https://www.mlh.com/events/100-days-of-solana/challenges)** challenge — every experiment, every write-up, every "aha" moment, documented here.

## 🚀 What is 100 Days of Solana?

The [MLH 100 Days of Solana](https://www.mlh.com/events/100-days-of-solana/challenges) challenge is a structured, day-by-day program that takes you from zero to Solana developer. It covers everything from keypair generation and wallet management to on-chain programs, tokens, and beyond.

## 💡 Week 1 Highlights

**The mobile wallet moment (Day 5)**
Coming in as someone who's worked on Ethereum and Polygon, I didn't expect a _mobile wallet_ to be my biggest surprise of the week. Setting up Phantom on mobile — with Google account recovery backed by iOS Keychain / Android Keystore — was the kind of UX that makes Web3 feel genuinely accessible. That's a bar Ethereum tooling hasn't consistently hit yet.

**Writing about what I know (Day 6)**
The challenge pushed me to write. Condensing a week of identity concepts into a single article — one that led with _"Meri chabi, meri pehchan"_ — was harder than expected and more satisfying than expected. Curiosity got me here; might as well keep going.
