## 📝 Overview

"Amplify Day" was about sharing my journey and connecting with the Solana community. While this wasn't my first time with blockchain (I've worked with Solidity, Ethereum, and Polygon before), it was my first hands-on week with Solana. I returned to blockchain dev after a year, driven by curiosity to try something new. The challenge: highlight a key moment from the week, post about it, and engage with others.

---

## 🛠️ Steps Followed

1. **Reflected on My Week**
   - Looked back at my previous blockchain experience and what was new with Solana.
   - Realized I already understood the concept of identity on blockchains, so nothing was truly "surprising"—but there were still two moments that stood out.
2. **Highlight #1: Mobile Wallets**
   - On Day 5, I compared wallet types and tried mobile wallets for the first time.
   - Was amazed by how easy it was to set up (even for non-techies) and that Phantom mobile supported wallet creation via Google account—making account recovery simple.
   - Learning about the use of iOS Keychain/Android Keystore for private key security made me feel safer using mobile wallets than other hot wallet options.
3. **Highlight #2: Writing My Day-6 Post**
   - I'm not a writer, but the challenge pushed me to condense my knowledge into a single write-up.
   - It was tough, but the process and the result were both satisfying.
4. **Created Social Posts**
   - Shared these highlights and my journey on:
     - [X (Twitter)](https://x.com/BhattDenil/status/2048399384778568129)
     - [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7454170162846441472/)
   - Used #100DaysOfSolana to join the conversation.
5. **Engaged with Others**
   - Read and commented on posts from fellow participants.
   - Amplified others' work by resharing and adding my thoughts.

---

## ✨ Key Takeaways

- **Mobile wallets are user-friendly and secure.** The ease of setup and use of secure device storage (Keychain/Keystore) was impressive.
- **Writing is powerful.** Summarizing my understanding in a public post was challenging but rewarding.
- **Learning in public** reinforces my knowledge and helps build connections.
- **On-chain identity** is a powerful, universal passport across Solana apps.
- **Community engagement**—feedback, questions, and encouragement—helps everyone grow.
